GATE ECE Exam Pattern:

The GATE ECE exam pattern consists of a 3-hour computer-based test with 65 questions carrying a total of 100 marks. The paper includes General Aptitude (15 marks) and Core Electronics Engineering subjects (85 marks), with MCQ, MSQ and Numerical Answer Type questions.

The GATE examination follows a structured pattern designed to evaluate a candidate’s technical knowledge, analytical ability, and problem-solving skills.

  • Mode of Examination

    • The GATE exam is conducted as a Computer-Based Test (CBT).
  • Duration:
    • The total duration of the examination is 3 hours.
  • Total Marks:
    • The question paper carries a maximum of 100 marks.
  • Number of Questions:
    • The exam usually contains 65 questions in total.
  • Sections in the Paper:
    • General Aptitude – 15 marks
    • Core Subject (chosen discipline) – 85 marks
  • Types of Questions:
    • Multiple Choice Questions (MCQ)
    • Multiple Select Questions (MSQ)
    • Numerical Answer Type (NAT)
  • Marking Scheme:
    • Questions may carry 1 mark or 2 marks.
    • Negative marking applies to certain MCQ questions.
    • MSQ and NAT questions generally do not have negative marking.
