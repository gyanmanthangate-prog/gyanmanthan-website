What is GATE ?

The Graduate Aptitude Test in Engineering (GATE) is a national-level examination conducted jointly by the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and the Indian Institute of Science (IISc) on behalf of the National Coordination Board (NCB), under the Ministry of Education, Government of India. The exam is designed to evaluate a candidate’s understanding of undergraduate-level subjects in engineering, technology, science, architecture, and related disciplines. Every year, thousands of engineering and science graduates appear for GATE to test their technical knowledge, analytical ability, and problem-solving skills.


A valid GATE score opens the door to several academic and career opportunities. It is widely used for admission to postgraduate programs such as M.Tech, M.E., MS, and PhD in prestigious institutes including IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other reputed universities across India. Candidates admitted to M.Tech or related postgraduate programs through GATE may also receive a monthly stipend of around ₹12,400 provided by the Government of India (subject to institute guidelines), which helps support students during their higher studies. In addition to higher education opportunities, many Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs) also consider GATE scores for recruiting engineers into technical roles. Because of these opportunities, GATE is considered an important examination for students who want to pursue advanced education, research, or technical careers in engineering and technology.
